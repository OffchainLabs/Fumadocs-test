## Child chain transactions

Generally, transactions executed through the <Term id="sequencer">Sequencer</Term> on Arbitrum chains [achieve finality](/docs/how-arbitrum-works/deep-dives/transaction-lifecycle.mdx) equivalent to their <Term id="parent-chain">parent chain</Term> once the relevant transaction data has been [posted in a batch](/docs/how-arbitrum-works/inside-arbitrum-nitro.mdx). This means that transactions on <Term id="arbitrum-chains">Arbitrum Chains</Term> are considered final in minutes.

## Parent chain → child chain transactions

Messages being sent through the <Term id="delayed-inbox">Delayed Inbox</Term> of a parent chain as [retryable tickets](/docs/how-arbitrum-works/deep-dives/l1-to-l2-messaging.mdx#retryable-tickets), including deposits through token bridges, are released by the [sequencer](/docs/launch-arbitrum-chain/configuration/sequencer) once it has reasonable confidence of finality on the parent chain. For example, on an <Term id="layer-2-l2">L2</Term> chain settling to Ethereum, the sequencer will release delayed messages to the inbox after 40 blocks. Following this, the transaction must complete another finality period for the Ethereum transaction that prompted it to achieve finality.

<Term id="arbitrum-chain">Arbitrum chain</Term> L3s may configure the finality of transactions executed through the Delayed Inbox to depend on different layers of finality. By default, Arbitrum chains will rely on the number of <Term id="layer-1-l1">L1</Term> block confirmations, effectively finalizing an L3 deposit as soon as L1 finalizes the <Term id="batch">batch</Term> posted by <Term id="arbitrum-one">Arbitrum One</Term> or when a <Term id="data-availability-certificate">DACert</Term> is posted by <Term id="arbitrum-nova">Arbitrum Nova</Term>. This would be on the order of tens of minutes.

However, in the instance of an L3 settling to Arbitrum One or Nova an L3 may also choose to rely only on L2 finality by configuring their sequencer as follows:

```
--node.delayed-sequencer.use-merge-finality=false
```

Additionally, the delay in L3 finalization can be decreased to achieve extremely fast (one minute) deposits by configuring the sequencer to wait for fewer <Term id="l2-block">L2 block</Term> confirmations:

```
--node.delayed-sequencer.finalize-distance=1
```

Note, however, that if you choose to enable fast bridging, a re-org of un-finalized blocks on the L3 may occur if Arbitrum One/Nova (or the settlement chain of choice) experiences a re-org.

## Child chain → parent chain transactions

Normally, [outgoing transactions](/docs/how-arbitrum-works/deep-dives/l2-to-l1-messaging.mdx) must wait until the <Term id="assertion">assertion</Term> that includes their L2 message is confirmed (~one week) before a <Term id="client">client</Term> can execute the message on L1. However, in the near future [AnyTrust](/docs/how-arbitrum-works/deep-dives/anytrust-protocol.mdx) chains will be able to leverage their DAC to enable fast confirmations of withdrawals through the native token <Term id="bridge">bridge</Term>. By immediately confirming assertions that have been signed by the DAC, finality can be reduced to ~15 minutes.
